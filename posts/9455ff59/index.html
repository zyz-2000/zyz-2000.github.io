Experiences

Study on DNA methyltransferase regulation of gene expression of Pseudomonas aeruginosa. (Master)

  • Sept. 2021 – Present
  • Project Leader
  • All methylation sites in Pseudomonas aeruginosa TBCF10839 and LYSZa7 were identified by SMRT-seq and corresponding R-M systems were identified by REBASE database. The molecular mechanism of DNA methyltransferase regulation of gene expression in Pseudomonas aeruginosa TBCF10839 and LYSZa7 was studied through transcriptome variation. Through site-directed mutation for the complete genome, the methylation sites that affect transcriptional regulation were identified. Build an SMRT sequencing data processing pipline, package it into software and generate a website.
  • Responsible for the construction of mutant strains, intracellular protein purification and secretory protein extraction, infection experiments, the third/second-generation sequencing data analysis.

    Study on regulation mechanism of bacterial type VI secretion system. (Master)

  • Sept. 2021 – Present
  • Project member
  • A transcriptional regulator was identified from the gene cluster of a type Ⅵ secretion system in Pseudomonas aeruginosa LYSZa7. Combined with phenotypic and transcriptomic evidence, this transcription factor also has regulatory effects on other genes outside the LYSZa7 type VI secretion system in Pseudomonas aeruginosa LYSZa7. The interaction between the transcription factor and other proteins was verified by Pull-Down and bacterial two-hybridization experiments.
  • Responsible for the construction of mutant strains, infection experiments, second-generation sequencing data analysis, analysis of gene homology and evolutionary tree mapping, and protein purification.

    Study on identification and functional analysis of small RNAs from Pseudomonas aeruginosa. (Master)

  • Sept. 2021 – Present
  • Project member
  • Transcriptome sequencing and differential expression analysis were used to compare the difference of RNA levels between Pseudomonas aeruginosa PAO1 under anaerobic and aerobic conditions, and a small RNA was screened and identified from the differentially expressed genes. Nanopore sequencing and Northern Blot were used to identify the small RNA and its major expression period. The length of the promoter region of the small RNA was determined by continuously reducing the length of its upstream promoter and constructing a fluorescent reporter gene. This small RNA was knocked out in P. aeruginosa PAO1 by gene knockout technology, and its effects on various phenotypes of P. aeruginosa PAO1 were determined. Multiple bioinformatics tools (TargetRNA, IntaRNA, CopraRNA) were used to predict small RNA targets, which were verified by EMSA. The nuclease that degrades this small RNA was predicted, purified and verified.
  • Responsible for the RNA-seq data analysis, Nanopore library construction and sequencing, construction of mutant strains and plasmid with reporter genes.

    Effect of desD gene on the synthesis of siderophore by endophytic actinomyces OsiSh-2. (Bachelor)

  • Mar. 2020 – Mar. 2021
  • Project member
  • The high siderophore-producing strain Osish-2 was isolated from plant leaf sheaths. desD gene was knocked out from OsiSh-2 and the effect of OsiSh-2 siderophore production on the interaction between OsiSh-2 and rice was verified.
  • Responsible for the construction of mutant strains, tobacco and rice cultivation.
